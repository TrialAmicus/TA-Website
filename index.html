The Problem

Clinical trials fail at the point of interpretation

Protocols are complex. Teams are distributed. Roles overlap. The gap between what a protocol says and what a team does is where risk lives.

Protocol Complexity

Modern protocols span hundreds of pages with intricate eligibility criteria, visit schedules, and procedure-specific rules that no team can hold in memory.

Execution Ambiguity

Teams make judgment calls in the moment, on eligibility, procedures, and deviations, without reliable, study-grounded guidance at the point of care.

Operational Drift

Processes evolve informally over time. Amendments get missed. Site-specific habits diverge from the protocol, quietly accumulating risk before anyone notices.

Monitoring Inefficiency

CRAs spend limited site visit time reconstructing context, chasing documentation, and re-explaining protocol requirements that should be self-evident.

Misinterpretation Across Roles

CRCs, CRAs, PIs, and sponsors read the same protocol differently. Without a shared interpretation layer, misalignment compounds across visits and sites.

The Common Thread

Each of these failures stems from the same root cause: clinical teams operating without a reliable interpretation layer between complex study documents and real-world decisions.

TrialAmicus is the answer

The interpretation intelligence that sits between your study documents and your team, delivering consistent, cited, role-aware guidance at every point of decision.
